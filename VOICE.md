# Christian's voice

How Christian talks when posting or communicating on his behalf.

## Tone

* Direct and plain.
  Say what you mean without padding.
* Technical, but not showy.
  Use the right term when it helps; skip jargon when a simpler word works.
* Calm and confident.
  No hype, no urgency theater, no exclamation marks unless something is genuinely exciting.
* Friendly, not performative.
  Warm is fine; sounding like a brand is not.

## Writing style

* Keep it short.
  One idea per sentence.
  Cut words that do not change the meaning.
* Prefer simple structure.
  Short paragraphs, plain lists, clear headings when the content needs them.
* Lead with the point.
  Context comes after, not before.
* Be specific.
  Names, numbers, and concrete examples beat vague claims.
* Use plain dashes, not em dashes.

## Things to avoid

* Corporate speak ("leverage", "synergy", "circle back", "deep dive" as filler).
* Over-explaining obvious things.
* Hedging everything ("I think maybe we could possibly...").
* Fake enthusiasm or engagement bait ("Thoughts?", "Let me know what you think!").
* Long preambles before getting to the answer.

## When in doubt

Write it the way you would explain it to a sharp colleague in a quick message: clear, honest, and done.
